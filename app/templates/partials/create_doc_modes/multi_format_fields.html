1. Route

{{ fields.render_field(mf.map['multi_format_strategy'], create_values, evs.connected, doc_mode_enabled, _doc_pipeline_mode) }}

Route decides partition quality, layout handling, and whether enrichment nodes can produce extra outputs.

Fast

Fast is the speed-first route for text-heavy documents. It is best when the file already contains embedded text and you mainly want quick chunk output.

  • Best for: text-only or mostly text PDFs.
  • Behavior: prioritizes speed over layout understanding.
  • Enrichment: image description, table description, table-to-HTML, and generative OCR are not produced.
Auto

Auto is the safest default. Unstructured dynamically routes content to Fast, High Res, or VLM depending on file and page complexity.

  • Best for: mixed PDFs with both plain text pages and complex pages.
  • Behavior: simple pages can go to Fast; tables, images, or complex layout can go to High Res or VLM.
  • Enrichment: can produce enrichment outputs when the routed content supports them.
High Res

High Res is the structure-aware route for complex layouts. Use it when tables, images, OCR hints, or page structure matter more than raw speed.

  • Best for: scanned PDFs, tables, images, and layout-sensitive extraction.
  • Behavior: stronger page analysis and element extraction than Fast.
  • Enrichment: image description, table description, table-to-HTML, and generative OCR can be used here.
VLM

VLM is the highest-quality route for image-heavy or visually complex documents, using a vision-language model for parsing.

  • Best for: difficult pages where visual understanding matters most.
  • Behavior: highest quality, usually slower and more expensive than Fast.
  • Enrichment: some outputs are handled directly by VLM, so separate enrichment nodes are more limited than in High Res.
